# Inspiration Brief — GiveHugz v4

## Page Architectures Studied

### 1. Rituals (rituals.com)
Full-bleed atmospheric hero (mood-first, not product-first) → editorial split sections → category discovery → values stamps → mission. The entire page reads like entering a spa. Every section transitions from emotional to commercial and back, never staying in sell-mode for consecutive sections.

### 2. Brooklinen (brooklinen.com)
Warm lifestyle hero with layered messaging → slim promo bar → horizontal category scroll → bold social proof moment ("150k+ reviews" in oversized serif) → trust bar (3 columns) → press logos → playful editorial break → education. Commerce-forward but personality-rich.

### 3. Aesop (aesop.com)
Magazine-cover homepage. Single editorial image opening → 3-4 products max shown → prose-like copy → architectural photography → literary content links. The lowest product density of any comparable brand. White space IS the design.

## Techniques to Steal

- **Page Flow** from Rituals: Alternating emotional/commercial sections. Warm immersion → trust signal → editorial story → bold punctuation → products → mission → invitation. Never two "sell" sections in a row.

- **Social Proof Treatment** from Brooklinen: One bold, oversized typography moment dedicated to social proof. Not a star-rating widget — a design moment. "50,000 families sleep better" set in 72px serif.

- **Product Restraint** from Aesop: Show 3-4 products as introductions, not a full catalog dump. Each product gets breathing room. Copy reads like meeting someone, not shopping.

- **Background Rhythm** from Brooklinen: Use warm cream ↔ white ↔ deep teal as section dividers (no border lines). One dark section mid-page as an anchor.

- **Typography Hierarchy** from Rituals: Micro-label (uppercase, tracked, 10px) → Large serif headline → Light-weight body → Understated CTA. Four distinct levels, never collapsed.

- **Mission Prominence** from Rituals: Values/mission in the bottom third but given the MOST dramatic visual treatment (darkest background, custom shapes, bold typography). The most "designed" section is the mission, not the products.

## The v4 Differentiator: Organic Warmth

Where v2 was "editorial warmth with asymmetric layouts," v4 pushes into organic territory:
- **Mesh gradients / organic blobs** in the hero — CSS-based, warm teal-to-cream-to-pink
- **Framer Motion scroll animations** — sections that breathe into existence, not snap
- **Parallax on product photography** — depth and tactility
- **Custom SVG shapes** that feel hand-drawn, not geometric
- **Section transitions** that flow like liquid, not stack like blocks

The goal: a site that feels like it was designed by a wellness brand, not an e-commerce brand.
