# Visual Research Notes

## 1. Rituals (rituals.com) — Spa/Wellness
**Screenshot**: rituals-desktop.png
**Steal**: The mood-first hero approach — full-bleed atmospheric lifestyle photography that sells a feeling before a product. The eyebrow label + large serif headline + understated CTA hierarchy. Asymmetric editorial splits. The alternating density rhythm (immersive → commercial → immersive). Generous vertical spacing as a luxury signal.

## 2. Aesop (aesop.com) — Premium Skincare
**Screenshot**: aesop-desktop.png (hit Cloudflare wall, but Aesop's design language is well-known)
**Steal**: Warm background tone (never pure white — always oat/cream). Literary voice in copy that reads like prose, not feature lists. Extreme restraint — low product density, no accent colors, quiet typography. White space as the primary design element. The "magazine cover" homepage approach.

## 3. Headspace (headspace.com) — Mindfulness App
**Screenshot**: headspace-desktop.png
**Steal**: Organic illustration style (rounded, blob-like shapes). Friendly but grounded tone — wellness without being clinical. Color as emotion (warm oranges/yellows = energy, cool blues = calm). The way they make an app feel tangible and warm.

## 4. Brooklinen (brooklinen.com) — Luxury Bedding
**Screenshot**: brooklinen-desktop.png
**Steal**: The bold social proof section ("150k+ 5-Star Reviews Don't Lie" in oversized serif). Horizontal category scroll pattern. Trust bar (3 columns, scannable). Background color as section divider (cream ↔ white ↔ navy). The playful editorial break mid-page. Warm wood/amber photography direction.

## 5. Calm (calm.com) — Meditation App
**Screenshot**: calm-desktop.png
**Steal**: The nature-as-metaphor approach (forests, water, landscapes as emotional shorthand). Deep, saturated color palette. How they present "calm" visually — dark blues, soft gradients, breathing-room layouts. Testimonials as storytelling.

## 6. Casper (casper.com) — Sleep Brand
**Screenshot**: casper-desktop.png
**Steal**: Sleep/comfort product presentation — warm bedroom scenes, soft lighting. How they handle a large product catalog while keeping it approachable. The "designed for rest" positioning overlap with weighted comfort products.

## 7. Godly.website — Design Curation
**Screenshot**: godly-reference.png
**Steal**: Current trends in web design — mesh gradients, organic blob shapes, creative grid breaks, bold typography treatments, scroll-triggered animations.
